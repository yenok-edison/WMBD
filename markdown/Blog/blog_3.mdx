# Transforming human RESOURCES into ASSETS


![Blog Details](/images/blogdetail-3.jpg)

In a typical setup, candidates apply for jobs and go through a few rounds of the interview process before being either selected or passed over. The primary factors that drive the selection of a candidate for a role or job in an organisation are qualifications, relevant experience, capabilities/skills/competencies and the individual's capacity to deliver. The team, comprising leaders from HR and domain teams, put in their best efforts to assess the candidate on the factors listed above, ascertain how well the candidate aligns with the job description/requirements and select the candidate for the job. 

After selecting the right candidate, many leaders have expressed a common frustration: the candidate looked perfect ‘ON PAPER’, but they are unable to deliver the expected performance.


**Why does this happen?**

My observation is that organisations look at hiring human resources aligned with a job description.  They do not put in the required effort to transform the resource into an asset (human asset). 

A human asset has the mindset “to find a way to make things happen,” going beyond skills, qualifications and competencies. It is the willingness to take initiative, the courage to own responsibility, the discipline to remain accountable and the commitment to deliver results without resorting to blame or excuses.

While knowledge and skills determine what a person can do; initiative, responsibility, accountability and commitment reflect what a person will do.

**Following are the qualities of a human asset:**

- Willingness to contribute
- Initiative and proactive action
- Ownership and accountability
- Responsibility without excuses
- Commitment to outcomes
- Resilience in the face of challenges.

---
These qualities are often overlooked during the hiring process because they are harder to measure than skills and qualifications. As a result, an organisation may end up selecting candidates who fit the role on paper but lack the mindset required to create value.

The real focus of leaders, therefore, should not be merely on hiring human resources but on identifying, nurturing and developing human assets who can consistently create impact and accelerate growth. 

The key focus of a leader is to develop resources into assets through constant nurturing and grooming.
